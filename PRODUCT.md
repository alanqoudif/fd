# Product

## Register

product

## Users

University and college students preparing for a computer hardware final exam. They study on phones between classes, on tablets at home, and on laptops for longer practice sessions. Many are bilingual (Arabic/English) and need conversion explanations in their preferred language.

## Product Purpose

A focused quiz app for computer hardware exam prep: MCQ and true/false practice, number-system conversion lessons with visual step-by-step explanations, progress tracking, and resume. Success means students can practice anywhere, understand mistakes immediately, and build confidence before the exam.

## Brand Personality

Clear, calm, trustworthy. Like a patient study partner — direct, encouraging, never flashy. Three words: focused, bilingual, supportive.

## Anti-references

Gamified edu apps with streaks and mascots. Flashcard clones with identical card grids. SaaS marketing aesthetics (cream backgrounds, gradient heroes). Over-animated UIs that slow down practice. Hiding core quiz features on mobile.

## Design Principles

1. **Task first** — every screen serves one job: start, answer, learn, or review results.
2. **Explain on failure** — wrong answers on conversion questions get immediate visual walkthroughs.
3. **Bilingual by choice** — Arabic UI shell, English question text where needed; explanation language is user-controlled.
4. **Trust through familiarity** — standard controls, predictable flows, no invented affordances.
5. **Practice anywhere** — full functionality on phone, tablet, and desktop without degraded mobile.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Minimum 44×44px touch targets on touch devices. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Sufficient contrast for body text and feedback states. Safe-area support for notched phones. Core flows usable with keyboard where applicable.
